NeurIPS 2025 Workshop

Self-Evolving AI in the Era of Experience

Links: NeurIPS 2025 | OpenReview

As we stand at the threshold of a new era in artificial intelligence, we are witnessing a fundamental shift from AI systems that distill human-priors toward agents that learn predominantly from experience. This workshop explores the emerging paradigm where AI systems transcend the limitations of human-generated data by developing capabilities through continuous interaction with their environments, memory formation, and self-evolutionary processes.

Recent breakthroughs, such as AlphaProof's achievement in the International Mathematical Olympiad and advances in reasoning systems like DeepSeek-R1, demonstrate the unprecedented potential of experiential learning. These systems generate orders of magnitude more training data through self-interaction than what exists in human-curated datasets, opening pathways to superhuman capabilities across diverse domains.

This workshop will bring together researchers, practitioners, and thought leaders to discuss the theoretical foundations, practical implementations, and societal implications of experiential AI systems. These systems learn from continuous streams of experience rather than episodic interactions, ground their actions and observations in rich environmental contexts, utilize rewards derived from environmental signals rather than human preferences alone, develop efficient machine-friendly reasoning patterns and planning strategies, and evolve their capabilities through self-directed learning processes. We hope to bridge the gap between current human-centric AI systems and truly autonomous intelligent agents by focusing on three central themes: experience-driven learning, persistent memory, and self-evolution.


Call for papers

Theme 1: Experience-Driven Learning:

We will address how AI systems can move beyond static training paradigms to continuous learning from environmental interaction. Key research questions include: How can we design architectures that learn efficiently from streams of experience rather than fixed datasets? What are the fundamental differences between episodic learning and continuous experiential learning? How can agents balance exploration and exploitation in open-ended environments? How do we ensure stable learning in non-stationary environments where the agent’s own actions change the world?

Theme 2: Persistent Memory:

We will explore how AI systems can accumulate and leverage long-term experiential knowledge while building predictive models of their environments. Critical research questions encompass: How can agents maintain and update persistent memory structures that span months or years of experience? What mechanisms allow for selective retention and forgetting of experiential knowledge while preserving privacy when human individuals are involved? How can systems integrate new experiences with existing knowledge while avoiding catastrophic forgetting? How can memory be internalized into world models that guide self-evolution? What architectures enable efficient retrieval and utilization of relevant past experiences for current decision-making, and how do we address data ownership and federated learning challenges?

Theme 3: Self-Evolution:

We will investigate algorithms that enable autonomous capability development and self-improvement, allowing systems to transcend their initial programming. Fundamental research questions include: What are the theoretical limits and possibilities of self-modifying AI systems? How can agents safely evolve their own learning algorithms and reward functions? How to balance rewards from internal reasoning and external interactions? What mechanisms prevent self-evolution from leading to capability collapse or misalignment? How can we ensure that self-evolving systems remain interpretable and controllable as they develop beyond their initial design?
